# M02 — Camelot Outskirts
*Version 1.0 | Status: ACTIVE | Planet: Belldor*

## 1. ZONE OVERVIEW
**The Camelot Outskirts** form the expansive perimeter outside the great walls of Camelot. What was once the Kingdom’s shining green breadbasket is now a war-torn, scarred landscape. Massive trench lines, broken siege engines, and overgrown supply routes dominate the terrain. 

**Atmosphere:** Desolate, oppressive, and heavily militarized. The skies are perpetually stained with the ash of burning pyres. The feeling is one of being in an active zone of conflict, where the remnants of Camelot’s knights wage an endless, senseless war against anyone who approaches or against hallucinations born from "Grail Sickness."

## 2. APW MAPPING PARADIGM
Applying the Anchored Procedural Wraparound (APW) rules to this zone:

### 2.1 Anchors
*   **White Anchor (Spawn):** *The Ruined Drawbridge.* The static entry point connected from the Camelot Border Town.
*   **Blue Anchor (Exit):** *The Outer Gates of Camelot.* A massive static gatehouse blocking entry to in-city Camelot. It is securely locked and requires three **Siege Commander Crests** to breach.

### 2.2 Colored Setpieces (Static Maps)
*   **Orange Key Setpiece:** *The Bloodied Jousting Fields.* A vast, muddy arena where Grail-Touched Knights endlessly duel. This is where the region's first major encounter takes place.
*   **Red Key Setpiece:** *The Sunken Cathedral.* A partially buried chapel functioning as a mini-dungeon. Contains one of the Siege Commander Crests. High density of Rotting Footmen and magical traps.
*   **Magenta Micro-Setpieces:**
    *   *The Broken Ballista:* A shattered siege engine overlooking a trench; usually spawns a random weapon cache.
    *   *The Deserter's Camp:* A small, hidden respite containing a healing font and lore fragment.

## 3. ENEMIES & AI MECHANICS (Belldor Encounter Design Law)
Encounter design emphasizes distinct mechanical counters rather than raw HP trading. Horizontal progression dictates that players must use specific feats or weapons to survive.

### 3.1 "Grail-Touched" Knights
Former elite guards driven mad by Bedivere’s Ring and the Grail's corrupting influence.
*   **Visuals:** Heavy armor emitting a sickly golden glow from the visors.
*   **Mechanic:** Possess a **Resonance Shield** that absorbs 100% of physical damage. Players must use *Impact* weapons to shatter their poise or use *Elemental* (Void/Fire) to bypass the shield.
*   **Behavior:** Relentless pursuit. They don't flinch until their shield is broken. 

### 3.2 Rotting Footmen
The rank-and-file soldiers left to rot in the trenches.
*   **Mechanic:** *Swarm & Pin.* They dive to grapple the player. If grappled, the player's stamina regeneration halts until they execute a breakout mechanic.
*   **Weakness:** Extremely vulnerable to wide sweeps and AoE Crowd Control feats (e.g., Marauder's "Whirlwind").

### 3.3 Siege Golems
Constructs built from debris and animated by corrupted magic.
*   **Mechanic:** *Slow but Devastating.* Their attacks create shockwaves that require timing dodges (i-frames) to avoid. Range attacks trigger their "Shield Wall", making them invulnerable from the front.
*   **Weakness:** The crystal core on their back. Requires positioning and mobility to exploit.

## 4. REGIONAL MECHANIC: "THE GRAIL'S GAZE"
An overarching environmental threat unique to the Camelot Outskirts. 

*   **The Threat:** Periodically, a massive beam of holy light (an orbital-strike-like artillery) sweeps the battlefield, targeting areas thick with conflict or player activity.
*   **The Tell:** A high-pitched, ringing sound grows louder, and the ground faintly illuminates with a golden reticle 3 seconds before impact.
*   **The Counter:** Players must break line of sight (duck into trenches, behind rocks, or inside ruined siege equipment) or time a perfect heavy dodge. 
*   **Strategic Use:** The blast affects enemies as well. Clever players (especially agile builds like the Rogue or tanking Guardians) can bait enemies into the impact zone to clear out overwhelming odds.

## 5. NOTABLE BOSS ENCOUNTER: COMMANDER BORS
The guardian of the Outer Gates, corrupted beyond recognition. Bors was a former Knight of the Round Table, now a towering monstrosity fused with his armor and wielding a massive halberd.

*   **Phase 1 — Honorable Duel:** Bors fights predictably, utilizing heavy sweeps and thrusts. Focus is on mastering parry timings.
*   **Phase 2 — Grail Sickness:** At 50% HP, Bors summons *The Grail's Gaze* continuously as a zone denial hazard while actively hunting the player. 
*   **Phase 3 — Desperation:** Bors shatters his halberd, using the pieces as dual-wielded frenzied weapons. Agility and perfect dodging are required over parrying.
*   **Loot:** *Bors's Broken Halberd* (Unlocks the "Halberdier's Stance" feat for Warrior/Marauder).

## 6. NPC INTERACTIONS & LORE
*   **The Sane Deserter:** Hidden in the trenches, a knight who avoided the Grail Sickness. He trades **Trench Rations** for information and provides cryptic hints on how to breach the Outer Gates.
*   **Lore Elements:** Notes found entirely within the trenches reveal that Bedivere’s Ring was not a protective artifact, but a conduit that chained the knights to the land, forbidding them from dying properly.

## 7. LOOT & PROGRESSION
*   **Resource Nodes:** *Scrap Iron*, *Corrupted Holy Water*, *Trench Mud*.
*   **Horizontal Progression (Discovery):** Finding the 3 Siege Commander Crests naturally guides the player through the static setpieces and introduces them to the core mechanics of the region, ensuring they have the mechanical knowledge needed for Bors and the city beyond.
