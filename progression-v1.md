## 1. CAMPAIGN ARC: THE BORDER TOWN (LEVELS 1–5)

The introductory arc focuses heavily on mechanical mastery and resource scarcity. The player does not begin as a fully-kitted operative; they begin with the bare minimum chassis loadout required for survival.

### 1.1 The "Empty Slots" Reality
All Action Bar and Equipment slots are physically present in the Combat UI at Level 1, but they are visibly empty. 
*   This creates an immediate psychological pull: the player can *see* the missing Twin-Links, Aux systems, and off-hand shields.
*   "Endgame" builds benchmarked around Level 20 involve fully populated slots and synergistic links. Level 1-5 builds are about tactical survival with single components.

### 1.2 Warrior L3 / L4 Loadout (Border Town Entry/Midpoint)
The following gear has been shifted out of the old "ideal default" and into the L5+ reward drop pools: 
*Gladius (Off-hand copy), Khopesh (Off-hand copy), Pilum, Buckler, Sensory Modules ×2, Repair Drone.*

**The Exact Loadout (L3-4):**
*   **Weapon Set 1:** Ichor Gladius (Bolter) [MAIN HAND ONLY, OFF-HAND EMPTY]
*   **Weapon Set 2:** Ichor Khopesh (Slash) [MAIN HAND ONLY, OFF-HAND EMPTY]
*   **Belt Slots:** Basic Booster Kit (Evade), 1× Healing Consumable.
*   **Aux/Modules:** Empty.

### 1.3 L3/L4 Action Bar State
With the above loadout, the Warrior interacts with combat using this heavily constrained Action State:
*   **Slot 1 (Ranged Attack):** Gladius basic combo chain.
*   **Slot 1 (Melee Attack):** Khopesh basic combo chain (when swapped).
*   **Slot 2 (Linked Skill / Off-hand):** EMPTY.
*   **Slot 3 (Guard):** Unarmed / Standard parry (highly inefficient vs impact, rapidly drains Stamina).
*   **Slot 4 (Evade):** Basic Booster Dash (consumes Energy).
*   **Slot 5+:** EMPTY.

## 2. BORDER TOWN BOSS FIGHT (OD-BOSS-01)
**Design Philosophy:** The L5 Boss isn't a complex DPS race against stacked cooldowns. It is a grueling, mechanical exam of spacing, heat management, and stamina conservation using the minimal toolkit.

*   **The Spacing Dictator:** The Boss dictates range. When it leaps away, the player *must* swap to the Gladius to return fire. When it rapidly closes the gap, the Gladius is inefficient; the player must snap-swap to the Khopesh to contend.
*   **Stamina Check (No Buckler):** Because the player has no Buckler, trying to block the Boss's heavy swings is disastrous (triggers rapid guard break/stun). The player *must* use their limited Energy to Booster-Dash perfectly away from telegraphed heavy attacks.
*   **The Heat Check:** During the ranged phase, holding down the Gladius trigger will overheat it, leaving the player vulnerable when the boss charges. Burst control is required.
*   **Reward (Camelot Threshold):** The boss's defeat marks the crossing of the L5 threshold into the Camelot hub. The reward pool includes the first major drops to fill the empty slots: the player's first **Buckler** or their second **Gladius** (enabling the first Class Link).
